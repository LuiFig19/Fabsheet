# 30-second iPad demo script (for the manager to narrate to the president)

Goal: show paper to job-costing in one smooth flow. Hand the iPad over already
open to the bookmarked URL. Each step is a tap; the line in quotes is what to
say while doing it.

---

**0. Start on the Dashboard** (already open from the home-screen bookmark)
> "This is our live job board. Hours this week, every job, and how each one is
> tracking against its budgeted hours. Green is under budget, red is over."

**1. Tap "Upload Timesheet"** (top right)
> "When a welder hands in their paper sheet, I just take a picture of it."

**2. Tap "Take photo"** the rear camera opens. Hold a real timesheet flat, snap it.
> "That's the actual paper sheet, nothing changes for the guys on the floor."

**3. Tap "Use photo"** the progress bar runs, then it reads the sheet.
> "It's reading the handwriting right now with AI. Job numbers, codes, hours."

**4. Land on the Review screen** (automatic, a few seconds later)
> "Here's what it pulled off the paper. Anything it wasn't sure about is
> highlighted yellow, so I check those instead of retyping the whole thing."

**5. Fix one highlighted cell, then tap "Approve all"**
> "I fix the one it flagged, approve, and it's locked in. Nothing counts until
> a manager approves it."

**6. Tap "Dashboard"** the job's progress bar has moved.
> "Those hours just landed on the job instantly. No spreadsheet, no re-entry."

**7. Tap "Reports", pick "This week", tap "Download PDF"**
> "And at the end of the week, this is the job-costing report the office gets,
> the same format they already use, as a PDF or a spreadsheet, or emailed
> straight to them."

**Close:**
> "Paper goes in, job costing comes out. It replaces the part where someone
> spends an hour every day typing these into the system by hand."

---

## Tips for a clean demo

- Pre-stage one real, filled-out timesheet to photograph.
- Make sure the Anthropic account has credits (or the read step will error).
- If the camera asks for permission the first time, tap Allow before the demo.
- Landscape or portrait both work; there is no horizontal scrolling.
- If the wifi is slow, the photo is auto-compressed before upload, so it is
  still quick.
